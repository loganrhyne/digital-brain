# Edna Mae's Sour Cream Pancakes

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Edna Mae's Sour Cream Pancakes.
- [claim] Source: Adapted from The Pioneer Woman Cooks.
- [claim] Original says 12 4-inch pancakes; makes about 8 5-inch pancakes.
- [claim] Can substitute some sour cream with yogurt.
- [claim] Be careful not to overmix - lumpy texture is okay.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 7 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 1 cup sour cream (can substitute some with yogurt)
- 2 large eggs
- 0.5 tsp vanilla extract
- Butter for cooking
- Maple syrup for serving

## Instructions
1. Heat cast iron skillet or griddle over medium-low heat; let it slowly get nice and hot.
2. Stir flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt together in medium bowl.
3. Dump sour cream on top and stir gently; it's okay to leave texture a bit uneven.
4. Whisk eggs and vanilla in separate bowl.
5. Stir eggs into sour cream mixture, being careful not to overmix.
6. Melt about 1 tbsp butter in skillet.
7. Pour batter in, a scant 1/4 cup at a time.
8. Cook about 2 minutes on first side, until bubbles appear all over surface.
9. Flip carefully and cook about 1 minute on other side.
10. Repeat with remaining batter.
11. Serve in a stack with butter and maple syrup.
