# Seafood "Who Needs Rice" Jambalaya

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Seafood "Who Needs Rice" Jambalaya.
- [claim] Posted April 26, 2010 by Sarah.
- [claim] Low-carb jambalaya made without rice.
- [claim] Can be spicy - feel free to omit hot sauce for more mild version.
- [claim] Pat fish and shrimp dry with paper towel for better texture after cooking.
- [claim] Sauté carrots for about 4 minutes, then add bell peppers and garlic for another 3-4 minutes.

- [claim] Author's family likes it spicy - even 2-year-old enjoys curries and salsa.

## Ingredients
- 1 lb wild Alaskan cod fillets (or other wild caught fish)
- 1 (16 ounce) bag frozen already cooked shrimp (thawed and drained)
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 green bell pepper, cut into strips
- 1 orange bell pepper, cut into strips
- 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
- 4-5 carrots, cut into strips
- A pinch or two of sea salt
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp fresh cracked pepper
- 4 cloves minced fresh garlic
- 1/4 cup organic butter or ghee
- Tapatio hot sauce (to taste)

## Instructions
1. Make sure your fish and shrimp are thawed and drained.
2. Pat the fish and shrimp dry with a paper towel.
3. Cut the fish into bite size pieces.
4. Melt butter in a large soup pot and sauté carrots for about 4 minutes.
5. Add the bell peppers and garlic and sauté for another 3-4 minutes.
6. Add all the spices and the chicken broth and bring to a boil.
7. Throw in the fish and simmer until the fish begins to flake.
8. Add the shrimp and stir just until the shrimp are warm. (If using fresh shrimp, throw in with the fish and cook for about the same amount of time together.)
9. Dowse with Tapatio and stir.
10. Serve in bowls immediately. Add more hot sauce if desired.
