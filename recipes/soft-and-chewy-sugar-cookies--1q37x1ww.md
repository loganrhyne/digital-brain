# Soft and Chewy Sugar Cookies

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Soft and Chewy Sugar Cookies.
- [claim] Source: Published November 2002.
- [claim] Use flour with ~10.5% protein (Pillsbury/Gold Medal). King Arthur (~11.7%) makes drier, cakier cookies.
- [claim] Dough balls freeze up to 1 week. Baked cookies keep 5 days in airtight container.
- [claim] Don't discard butter wrappers - use for greasing the glass.
- [todo] Original has missing ingredients list.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- All-purpose flour (Pillsbury or Gold Medal preferred)
- Baking powder
- Salt
- Butter
- 1 cup granulated sugar (plus more for rolling)
- Brown sugar
- 1 egg
- Vanilla

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F with racks in upper and lower-middle. Line baking sheets with parchment.
2. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt in medium bowl.
3. Beat butter and sugars until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
4. Add egg and vanilla, beat until combined.
5. Add dry ingredients and beat at low speed until just combined.
6. Dip hands in water, shake off excess. Roll heaping tablespoon of dough into 1.5-inch balls.
7. Roll balls in sugar, place 2 inches apart on sheets (12 per sheet).
8. Butter bottom of drinking glass with butter wrapper, dip in sugar, flatten balls to 3/4-inch thick.
9. Bake 15-18 minutes until golden around edges and just set in center, rotating sheets halfway.
10. Cool on sheets 3 minutes, then transfer to wire rack.
