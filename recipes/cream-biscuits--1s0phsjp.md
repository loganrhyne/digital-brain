# Cream Biscuits

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Cream Biscuits.
- [claim] Source: Adapted from James Beard's American Cookery.
- [claim] Line baking sheets with parchment instead of buttering to avoid smoking.
- [claim] Can be flash frozen for future use; bake from frozen with 3-5 extra minutes.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 3 tbsp melted butter
- 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
- 1 tbsp baking powder
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
- 1.5 cups heavy cream

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (215°C). Line baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Melt butter in small pot or microwave, set aside.
3. Sift flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar (if using) into large bowl.
4. Fold in 1 1/4 cups cream. If dough is not soft or easily handled, fold in remaining 1/4 cup cream little by little.
5. Turn dough onto floured surface, mound into a ball.
6. Using hands, press to thickness of about 3/4 inch.
7. Cut into 2.5-inch rounds. Gather scraps and continue making rounds.
8. Dip top of each round in melted butter and arrange on baking sheet.
9. Bake until golden, 12-15 minutes.
10. Serve immediately or flash freeze for future use.
