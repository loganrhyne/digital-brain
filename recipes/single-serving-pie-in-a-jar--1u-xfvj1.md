# Single Serving Pie in a Jar

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Single Serving Pie in a Jar.
- [claim] Use half-pint short/squatty jars (Kerr brand works well).
- [claim] Goes straight from freezer to oven to mouth.
- [claim] No need to grease jars.
- [claim] Can use any pie dough or store-bought crust.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients

### Dough
- Pie dough (enough for 4 jars - about 1 recipe basic crust)

### Filling (for 4 pies)
- 2 cups prepared fruit (pitted, diced, peeled as needed)
- 2 tbsp sugar (brown or white - adjust to fruit sweetness)
- 2 tbsp flour (more for juicy fruit, less for dry)
- 1 tbsp butter (divided between pies)
- Seasonings: cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla, almond extract, citrus zest

## Instructions
1. Roll out small portion of dough. Use jar ring as cookie cutter for tops. Set aside.
2. Line jars with remaining dough - just press small pieces in. Press all the way to top.
3. Combine fruit, sugar, flour, and seasonings.
4. Divide filling between jars (about 1/2 cup each).
5. Dot with butter (about 1/4 tbsp per jar).
6. Cut vents in tops (slits or decorative cutouts).
7. Place tops on pies, pressing edges against jar sides.
8. Brush with egg wash, sprinkle with coarse sugar.

### To Freeze
9. Place jars on baking sheet, freeze until solid. Store in freezer bags.
