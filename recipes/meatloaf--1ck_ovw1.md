# Meatloaf

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Meatloaf with two variations.
- [claim] First version uses grass fed ground beef with almond meal (paleo-friendly).
- [claim] Second version uses salsa and cheese for flavor.
- [claim] Second version yields approximately 190 calories, 31g protein, 3g carb, 1g fiber, 6g fat, 687mg sodium per slice.
- [claim] Doesn't bind as well as regular meatloaf with grain, but still works well.
- [claim] Pairs well with a salad (feta suggested).
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients

### Version 1: Paleo Meatloaf
- 1 diced red onion
- 2 lbs grass fed ground beef
- 1 cup almond meal
- 2 eggs
- 1 can tomato paste
- 1 tbsp crushed garlic
- 1/2 tbsp sea salt
- 2 tbsp dried basil
- 1 tsp marjoram (appears truncated)
- Cracked black pepper to taste

### Version 2: Salsa & Cheese Meatloaf
- 1.5 lbs ground beef
- 1/2 cup chunky salsa (fat free), divided
- 1 egg white, beaten lightly
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 3/4 tsp ground cumin
- 3/4 tsp salt
- 1 cup shredded cheese (fat free)

## Instructions

### Version 1: Paleo Meatloaf
1. Mix all ingredients by hand in a large mixing bowl.
2. Place meat mixture into a large glass baking pan and form into a loaf.
3. Bake at 350°F for 1 hour or until meat is no longer pink in the middle.

### Version 2: Salsa & Cheese Meatloaf
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease 4x8 inch loaf pan.
2. Combine beef, 1/4 cup of salsa, egg and spices in bowl, mixing until blended.
3. Move half of beef mixture to pan. Top with cheese, leaving 1/2 inch border along edge.
4. Put remaining beef mixture on top. Spread remaining 1/4 cup salsa on top.
5. Bake until thermometer inserted into center shows 160°F (65-70 minutes).
6. Let stand for five minutes. Cut into six slices and serve.
