# Three Cities of Spain Cheesecake

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Three Cities of Spain Cheesecake.
- [claim] Source: Gourmet Magazine, November 1999.
- [claim] Created by Santa Fe's Three Cities of Spain coffeehouse (closed mid-1970s).
- [claim] Considered the absolute favorite in the creamy category.
- [claim] Keeps covered and chilled 3 days.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients

### Crust
- Graham cracker crumb crust (finely ground)

### Filling
- 3 (8-oz) packages cream cheese, softened
- 4 large eggs
- 1 tsp vanilla
- 1 cup sugar

### Topping
- 16 oz sour cream
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp vanilla

## Instructions
1. Make crumb crust and press into springform pan. Preheat oven to 350Â°F.
2. Beat cream cheese with electric mixer until fluffy.
3. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla and sugar, beating on low speed until each is incorporated. Scrape bowl between additions.
4. Put springform pan in shallow baking pan. Pour filling into crust.
5. Bake in middle of oven 45 minutes, until set 3 inches from edge but center still slightly wobbly.
6. Let stand in baking pan on rack 5 minutes. Leave oven on.
7. Stir together sour cream, sugar, and vanilla for topping.
8. Drop spoonfuls around edge of cake, spread gently over center.
9. Bake with topping 10 minutes.
10. Run knife around top edge to loosen. Cool completely on rack.
11. Chill loosely covered at least 6 hours.
12. Remove pan side, transfer to plate. Bring to room temperature before serving.
