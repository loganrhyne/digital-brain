# Pesto

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Pesto.
- [claim] Makes 3/4 cup, enough for 1 pound of pasta.
- [claim] Pounding basil releases flavorful oils more readily.
- [claim] Can substitute 1 tbsp pecorino Romano for 1 tbsp Parmesan for sharper flavor.
- [claim] Keeps refrigerated 4 days or frozen 1 month (cover with thin layer of oil).

## Ingredients
- Pine nuts or walnuts
- 2-3 garlic cloves
- 2 cups fresh basil leaves
- Fresh parsley (optional, for color)
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 1/2 tsp salt
- Parmesan cheese (freshly grated)
- Black pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Toast nuts in small heavy skillet over medium heat, stirring frequently, until golden and fragrant (about 5 minutes). Set aside.
2. Toast garlic in empty skillet over medium heat, shaking occasionally, until fragrant and color deepens slightly (about 7 minutes). Cool, peel, and chop.
3. Place basil and parsley (if using) in heavy-duty 1-gallon zipper-lock bag. Pound with flat side of meat pounder or rolling pin until leaves are bruised.
4. Process nuts, garlic, herbs, oil, and 1/2 tsp salt in food processor until smooth (about 1 minute), scraping down sides as needed.
5. Stir in Parmesan and season with salt and pepper to taste.
