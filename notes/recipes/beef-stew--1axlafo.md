# Beef Stew

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Beef Stew.
- [claim] Serves 8 (about 1 cup per serving).
- [claim] Sauté mushrooms for 5 minutes until beginning to brown.
- [claim] Sauté onions for 10 minutes until tender and golden brown.
- [claim] Dredge beef in flour before browning.
- [claim] Brown beef in batches for 6 minutes per batch.
- [claim] Simmer covered for 1 hour until beef is just tender.
- [claim] Add potatoes and carrots and simmer uncovered for 1 hour 15 minutes until very tender and sauce is thick.
- [claim] Discard bay leaf before serving.
- [claim] Garnish with fresh thyme sprigs if desired.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon olive oil, divided
- 1 pound small cremini mushrooms
- Cooking spray
- 2 cups chopped onion
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/3 cup all-purpose flour (about 1 1/2 ounces)
- 2 pounds lean beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 3/4 teaspoon salt, divided
- 1 cup dry red wine
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
- 2 (14-ounce) cans less-sodium beef broth
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 cups (3/4-inch) cubed peeled white potato (about 1 pound)
- 1 1/2 cups (1-inch) slices carrot (about 12 ounces)
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- Fresh thyme sprigs (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat 1 teaspoon olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms, and sauté for 5 minutes or until mushrooms begin to brown. Spoon mushrooms into a large bowl.
2. Lightly coat pan with cooking spray. Add onion; sauté 10 minutes or until tender and golden brown. Add garlic; sauté 1 minute. Add onion mixture to mushroom mixture.
3. Place flour in a shallow bowl or pie plate. Dredge beef in flour, shaking off excess.
4. Heat remaining 2 teaspoons oil in pan over medium-high heat. Add half of beef mixture; sprinkle with 1/8 teaspoon salt. Cook 6 minutes, browning on all sides. Add browned beef to mushroom mixture.
5. Repeat procedure with remaining beef mixture and 1/8 teaspoon salt.
6. Add 1 cup wine to pan, scraping pan to loosen browned bits. Add thyme, broth, and bay leaf; bring to a boil.
7. Stir in beef mixture. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 1 hour or until beef is just tender.
8. Stir in potato and carrot. Simmer, uncovered, 1 hour and 15 minutes or until beef and vegetables are very tender and sauce is thick, stirring occasionally.
9. Stir in remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt and pepper. Discard bay leaf.
10. Garnish with thyme sprigs, if desired.
