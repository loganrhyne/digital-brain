# Norwegian Butter Cookies (Spritz)

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Norwegian Butter Cookies (Spritz).
- [claim] Source: Gourmet, March 1983; reprinted December 2001.
- [claim] Adapted from Carrie Young.
- [claim] Makes about 6 dozen cookies.
- [claim] If no cookie press, can form into 1 1/2-inch balls and flatten with fork to make crosshatch design.
- [claim] Cookies keep in airtight container at room temperature 3 weeks.
- [claim] Bake until edges are golden, 10 to 15 minutes.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
- Rounded 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 3 sticks (1 1/2 cups) unsalted butter, softened
- 1 cup sugar
- 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
- 1 large egg, lightly beaten

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350Â°F.
2. Sift together flour, baking powder, and salt.
3. Beat together butter, sugar, and extracts in a large bowl with an electric mixer at moderately high speed until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
4. Add egg and beat well.
5. Add flour mixture and mix at low speed until just combined.
6. Quarter dough and put 1 piece into cookie press fitted with a star, bow, circle, or ribbon plate.
7. Holding press slightly above ungreased baking sheets, squeeze out dough to form cookies (follow manufacturer's instructions), spacing them about 3 inches apart.
8. Form more cookies with remaining dough in same manner.
9. Bake cookies in batches in middle of oven until edges are golden, 10 to 15 minutes.
10. Transfer with a metal spatula to racks to cool.
