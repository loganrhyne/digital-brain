# Shipa's Dal and Rice with Ginger and Green Chilies

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Shipa's Dal and Rice with Ginger and Green Chilies.
- [claim] Serves 4-5.
- [claim] Can use yellow split peas or toor dal.
- [claim] Add garlic, chilies, and ginger one at a time, cooking for a minute or two between each.
- [claim] Cook onions until clear(ish) and fragrant before adding water, dal, and rice.
- [claim] Cook for 40 minutes or until dal and rice is tender and water is absorbed.
- [claim] Stir occasionally to avoid scorching.
- [claim] Allow to cool for a few minutes if you can wait - aroma is irresistible.
- [claim] Humorous note: "I have the burnt tongue to prove it."
- [claim] Use fewer green chilies for a milder dish.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 1 cup yellow split peas (or toor dal)
- 1 cup brown basmati rice
- 1 tbsp canola oil
- 1 large yellow onion, chopped
- 6-8 cloves garlic, crushed and minced
- 5-6 green chilies, finely chopped (small, pinky-finger-sized; use fewer for milder dish)
- 4" x 1" knob ginger, grated (about 3-4 tbsp)
- 6 cups water
- Salt to taste

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a stock pot or large saucepan.
2. Add the onion and saut√© until soft.
3. One at a time, add garlic, chilies, and ginger, cooking for a minute or two between each addition.
4. When the onions are clear(ish) and fragrant, add the water, dal, and rice.
5. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and cover.
6. Cook for 40 minutes or until dal and rice is tender and water is absorbed. Stir occasionally to avoid scorching.
7. Allow to cool for a few minutes before serving.
