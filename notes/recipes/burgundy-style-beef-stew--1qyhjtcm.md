# Burgundy-style Beef Stew

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Burgundy-style Beef Stew.
- [claim] Simple dish requiring long, slow cooking.
- [claim] Marinating beef several days ahead is key to success.
- [claim] Well worth the effort - end result is delicious.
- [claim] Marinate overnight in refrigerator, turning meat once or twice.
- [claim] Cook covered at 150°C for 2 hours, then add salt and cook 1 more hour.
- [claim] Add mushrooms in final 30 minutes.
- [claim] Important to buy meat as steaks then cut into large pieces to retain juiciness.
- [claim] Less tender cuts like gravy beef, chuck steak, blade or skirt are most suitable.
- [claim] Expensive cuts are wasted and toughened by long slow cooking.
- [claim] Use New Zealand cabernet sauvignon or merlot for marinating, or half wine and stock.
- [claim] Need heavy-based casserole with tight-fitting lid (about 4 liters).
- [claim] New pans may need to be seasoned before first use.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- Gravy beef, chuck steak, blade or skirt (quantity not specified, about 4cm pieces)
- 1 carrot, peeled and cut into chunky pieces
- 1 onion, peeled and cut into chunky pieces
- Celery, cut into chunky pieces
- Fresh herbs (thyme, parsley, bay leaf for bouquet garni)
- Red wine (New Zealand cabernet sauvignon or merlot)
- Black pepper
- Salt
- Butter or spread (for thickening)
- Flour (for thickening)
- Button mushrooms, cleaned
- Chopped parsley for serving (optional)

## Instructions
1. Trim sinew from the gravy beef. Cut the beef into about 4cm pieces and put into a heavy casserole dish.
2. Peel and cut the carrot, onion and celery into chunky pieces and add, along with the herbs.
3. Pour the red wine over the meat and vegetables and grind in the black pepper.
4. Cover and put in the refrigerator overnight, turning the meat once or twice with a big spoon to ensure even coverage.
5. About 3 1/2 hours before you want to eat, heat the oven to 150°C and cook the covered casserole for 2 hours.
6. Remove, stir in the salt, add more pepper if you wish and cook for a further 1 hour.
7. To thicken the gravy, combine the butter or spread and flour together to a smooth mix. Thin with a spoonful of hot stew liquid and stir back into the stew.
8. Add the cleaned button mushrooms and cook for another 30 minutes.
9. Scatter each serving with chopped parsley if you wish.
