# Whole Wheat Brioche

## Observations
- [definition] Recipe for Whole Wheat Brioche.
- [claim] Uses white whole wheat flour for lighter color and flavor.
- [claim] Dough can be stored and used over 5 days.
- [claim] Vital wheat gluten helps with structure.
- [question] What substitutions work well?

## Ingredients
- 4 cups white whole wheat flour
- 3 cups unbleached all-purpose flour
- 1.5 tbsp granulated yeast
- 1 tbsp kosher salt
- 0.25 cup vital wheat gluten
- 2.25 cups lukewarm water
- 0.75 cup (1.5 sticks) unsalted butter, melted (or margarine or neutral oil)
- 0.75 cup honey
- 5 large eggs

### Egg Wash
- 1 egg beaten with 1 tbsp water

## Instructions
1. Whisk together flour, yeast, salt, and vital wheat gluten in 5-quart bowl.
2. Combine liquid ingredients and mix with dry ingredients without kneading.
3. Dough will be loose but will firm up when chilled.
4. Cover (not airtight) and let rest at room temperature until it rises and collapses, about 2 hours.
5. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before using. Can be stored up to 5 days.
6. On baking day, grease a brioche mold or 8.5x4.5-inch loaf pan.
7. Dust dough with flour, cut off 2-pound (cantaloupe-size) piece. Shape into ball.
8. Place in prepared pan. Rest loosely covered for about 1 hour 45 minutes.
9. Preheat oven to 350Â°F with rack in middle.
10. Brush with egg wash.
11. Bake near center of oven for 40-45 minutes.
12. Remove from pan and cool on rack before slicing.
